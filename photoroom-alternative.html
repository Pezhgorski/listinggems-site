Why people are searching for a Photoroom alternative

Photoroom is a capable tool. It’s also the most-complained-about background remover in the reseller community right now. The common reasons sellers leave:

Cost

The monthly fee never ends

Pro is $12.99/month ($156/year). Max is $34.99/month ($420/year). Ultra goes to $99/month. Whether you sell 5 items this month or 500, the subscription bills every month. Three years on Pro is $468 — almost 10× the price of ListingGems. Most sellers don’t process photos every single day, but the credit card does.

Licensing

The free tier doesn’t allow selling

Photoroom’s free plan explicitly prohibits commercial use. Every photo you export and put on an Amazon, Etsy, eBay, or Poshmark listing is a paid use case — technically a ToS violation if you’re on Free. The “free” tier is functionally a trial for hobbyists. ListingGems’ $49 license includes full commercial use on 3 devices, no upgrade path required.

Privacy

Every photo gets uploaded

Photoroom is a cloud tool. Every image you process leaves your machine and lands on their servers for AI inference. The privacy policy URL on photoroom.com returns 404 at time of writing — image-retention period and training-data opt-out are not clearly documented. If you sell private-label, vintage with provenance, or unreleased designs, those photos are on someone else’s computer.

Caps

Image limits per tier

Pro = 500/month, Max = 1,500/month. Big-batch sellers, crosslisting resellers, and anyone bulk-processing a thrift haul hits the wall. Going over means upgrading or waiting until next month.

Trust

The November 2025 retroactive cap

Customers who pre-paid for “unlimited” Pro and Max plans had their accounts capped mid-term at 500 and 1,500 images/month, with no refunds. Documented in an open letter on Medium. Trustpilot rating sits at 1.3/5 across ~194 reviews.

Billing

Cancellation & refund friction

Recurring complaints across Trustpilot and Capterra: auto-renew after free trial, accounts disabled before the billing period ended, refunds denied, support that loops you through bots. Multiple users report needing card chargebacks.

Quality

Quality reportedly getting worse

Long-time users on r/Flipping report the output has degraded over time even as prices rose. Reviews mention over-erased hair and edges, and pixels that don’t match the real product when AI scene generation is on. Marketplace listing photos need to look like the actual product.

Reliability

Batch processing crashes at scale

Resellers report that batch uploads crash at 150–180 photos even when paying for 250-image batches. A Facebook reseller group post asked if others were seeing it too. If a 250-photo batch fails at photo 170, you re-upload everything — or you split into smaller batches that defeat the point.

Support

Support loops through bots

Capterra and Trustpilot reviews repeatedly describe a 2-day human response time and AI chatbots that loop without resolving billing issues. “The only contact is a robot” appears in recent reviews. For sellers chasing refunds on retroactive cap changes, that’s a closed door.

Platform

Mobile-first, weak on desktop

Photoroom is strongest on iOS/Android. Native desktop is M1-Mac only; Windows and older Macs are stuck with the web app. Sellers managing 50–500 SKUs from Seller Central or Shopify on a desktop find the workflow clunky.

Scope

You still need other tools

Photoroom is mainly background removal + AI scene generation. For exposure / white balance fixes, 4× upscaling, or marketplace-specific export dimensions, most sellers reach for a second app. That’s another tab, another login, often another subscription.

When Photoroom is still the right call

Honest tradeoffs. Not every seller should switch.

  • You work primarily on a phone. Photoroom has solid iOS and Android apps. ListingGems is desktop only (Windows, Linux, macOS coming).
  • You need AI-generated lifestyle backgrounds. Photoroom can hallucinate scenes (cafes, beaches, marble tables). ListingGems intentionally won’t — it’s built for marketplace-compliance, not stylized social content.
